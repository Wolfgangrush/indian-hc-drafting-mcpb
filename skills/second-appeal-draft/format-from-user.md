```
IN THIS HON'BLE COURT OF JUDICATURE AT BOMBAY BENCH AT NAGPUR.

SECOND APPEAL NO.                 /<YEAR>
ACT CODE NO.                     .

(Being aggrieved by the Judgment and Order dated <date1> passed by the learned <first appellate court / lower court> in <case-no1> thereby <action — e.g., "rejecting the application for condonation of delay">, and the Judgment and Decree dated <date2> passed by the learned <trial court> in <case-no2>)

APPELLANT:
(Original Plaintiff/ Applicant)


<Name> S/o / D/o <Father's Name>,
Aged about <age> years, Occ: <occupation>, R/o <address>

//Versus//

RESPONDENTS:
(Orig. Defendants/ Non-Applicants)
1.
<Name 1>, Aged <N> Yrs, Occ: <occupation>

2.
<Name 2>, Aged <N> Yrs, Occ: <occupation>

[Where a respondent is deceased and prosecuted through legal heirs:]
3.
<Original Name> (Dead) through his legal heirs:

  3a.
  <Heir Name>, Aged <N> Yrs, Occ: <occupation>

  3b.
  <Heir Name>, Aged <N> Yrs, Occ: <occupation>

  [...continue through all heirs]

[All respondents below grouped by common address:]
Respondent Nos. 1, 2, 3-a to 3-c & R-4
R/o <common address>


SECOND APPEAL UNDER SECTION 100 OF THE CODE OF CIVIL PROCEDURE, 1908.

 	This instant Second Appeal is arising out of the Judgment and Order dated <date1> passed by the learned <first appellate court>, in <case-no1> ANNEXURE-A, thereby <action>, and the Judgment and Decree dated <date2> passed by the learned <trial court> in <case-no2>.

(For the purpose of claim and jurisdiction of this Hon'ble Court, Court Fees of Rs. <amount>/- is paid, same as paid before the lower courts).


MOST RESPECTFULLY SHEWETH

 	Being aggrieved by the Judgment and Order dated <date1> passed by the learned <first appellate court> in <case-no1>, and the Judgment and Decree dated <date2> passed by the learned <trial court> in <case-no2>, the Appellant begs to prefer the instant Second Appeal on the following facts and grounds.


F A C T S
  •	 	It is submitted that the present dispute involves <subject matter description — e.g., "the ancestral agricultural land bearing Gat No. <N>, ad-measuring <area>, situated at <village>">.

  •	It is submitted that, the Appellant and the Respondents are <relationship description — e.g., "the legal heirs of the deceased <Name>">. <Genealogy / family tree facts establishing the dispute.>

  •	It is submitted that the Appellant/Plaintiff <prior procedural history — e.g., "had previously filed <prior case-no> for partition and separate possession of the suit property, which came to be dismissed on <date>">.

  •	Consequently, the Appellant instituted <next-step action — e.g., "a fresh suit for partition and separate possession, registered as <case-no>">, before the Court of <trial court>, praying for <relief>. The copy of the Plaint submitted in the Trial Court in <case-no> is annexed herewith and marked as ANNEXURE-B.

  •	The learned <trial court designation> (Presided over by <judge>), vide Judgment and Decree dated <date>, dismissed <case-no>. The Trial Court held that <ratio of trial court — case-specific>. The copy of the Trial Court Judgment and Decree dated <date> in <case-no> is annexed herewith and marked as ANNEXURE-C.

  •	Being aggrieved by the Trial Court's judgment, the Appellant <appellate procedural history — e.g., "sought to prefer an appeal before the District Court. However, due to <reason>, an inadvertent delay occurred in preferring the appeal. The Appellant ... filed an application under Section 5 of the Limitation Act for condonation of delay, which was registered as <case-no> before the <appellate court>">.

  •	The learned <first appellate court designation>, vide Judgment and Order dated <date>, <action — e.g., "rejected the Appellant's application for condonation of delay">. The Appellate Court observed that <ratio of appellate court — case-specific>. The copy of the Impugned Order passed by the Appellate Court in <case-no> dated <date> is annexed herewith and marked as ANNEXURE-A.

  •	[Closing fact paragraph explaining the procedural posture and why direct approach to HC by Second Appeal is appropriate.]


SUBSTANTIAL QUESTIONS OF LAW

  •	Whether the learned <First Appellate Court / Trial Court> committed a substantial error of law in <action> in <case-no> vide the impugned order dated <date>, by <legal characterisation of the error — e.g., "adopting a hyper-technical and pedantic approach rather than a liberal and justice-oriented approach">, thereby <consequence — e.g., "unjustly denying the Appellant her statutory right to have her First Appeal heard on merits">?

  •	Whether the learned <court> committed error in appreciating <legal concept / phrase / principle — case-specific> as defined in / under <statutory provision>?

  •	Whether the learned <court> erred in law and committed a jurisdictional error in <action — case-specific> without <required step — e.g., "framing issue on the point of Res Judicata">?

  •	Whether the learned <court> failed to appreciate the settled legal position that <legal principle being invoked — case-specific>?

  •	Whether the <court> committed a patent error in applying <statutory provision — case-specific> without strictly verifying whether <required pre-condition>?

  •	Whether the cumulative effect of <combined errors> has resulted in a grave miscarriage of justice, permanently depriving the Appellant — <appellant character — e.g., "a lone, uneducated agriculturalist"> — of <right being denied>?

  [Each question framed as "Whether..." with case-specific legal substance. SA u/s 100 maintainability requires SQL — no case-specific framing = no SA.]


  •	The Appellants have not filed any other Appeal in the subject matter either before this Hon'ble Court or before Supreme Court of India.

  •	The Appellants craves leave to add or amend the Appeal if necessary. The Appellant craves leave to file more documents if necessary.

  •	The Appellants undertakes to file the translated copies of the vernacular documents as and when directed by this Hon'ble Court.


P R A Y E R:

It is therefore, most humbly and respectfully prayed that this Hon'ble Court may kindly be pleased to –

a)
Call the Record and Proceedings of <case-no2> decided by the <trial court> and call the record of <case-no1> decided by learned <first appellate court>;

b)
Upon Perusal of the same, set aside the Impugned Order dated <date1> passed by the Learned <first appellate court> ANNEXURE-A, in <case-no1>, and the Judgment and Decree dated <date2> passed by the learned <trial court> in <case-no2> ANNEXURE-C;

c)
Remand the matter back to the lower appellate court to register the <Regular Civil Appeal / appropriate matter> and decide the same on its own merits, OR alternatively, decree <case-no2> filed by the Appellant for <substantive relief>;

d)
Grant stay to the execution/operation of the decrees passed by the learned <courts> till the decision of this appeal;

e)
Grant ex-parte ad-interim relief in terms of prayer clause no. (d) above;

f)
Grant such other appropriate relief which this Hon'ble Court deems fit and proper in the facts and circumstances of the case.


NAGPUR
DATE:<DD.MM.YYYY>
COUNSEL FOR APPELLANT
(<COUNSEL NAME>)
```

## SECTION ORDER IN FINAL .docx

For Second Appeal, the single .docx contains, in this order:
1. Index
2. Synopsis
3. Main Appeal (Cause Title → Parties → Statutory Opening → Court Fees Note → Salutation → FACTS → SUBSTANTIAL QUESTIONS OF LAW → Standard Closing Items → PRAYER → Counsel block)
4. List of Annexures
5. [Optional] Stay Application (where interim relief sought separately)

Note ordering difference from Criminal Appeal: SA places Index/Synopsis BEFORE Main Appeal in some chambers' practice. the user's the Second Appeal master template suggests Synopsis-then-Main is preferred for SA.

## NOTES FOR DRAFTER

- **SUBSTANTIAL QUESTIONS OF LAW is the central section** — replaces "GROUNDS" of appeals/petitions. Maintainability requires at least one SQL clearly framed.
- **Court header variation:** SA uses "IN THIS HON'BLE COURT" (note "THIS" + "HON'BLE") rather than Cri Appeal's "IN THE HIGH COURT".
- **Parties separator:** `//Versus//` (double slashes lowercase) on civil side, vs `///VERSUS///` on criminal side.
- **Salutation:** civil side uses "MOST RESPECTFULLY SHEWETH" instead of criminal-side "humbly and respectfully begs to submits".
- **Court fees note:** appears on civil-side after statutory opening — "Court Fees of Rs. <amount>/- is paid, same as paid before the lower courts".
- **Counsel block ordering:** SA template shows "COUNSEL FOR APPELLANT" line BEFORE counsel name. Criminal Appeal shows counsel name BEFORE "COUNSEL FOR APPELLANT". Both are the user's actual practice — preserve as-is per case type.
- **SQL framing rule:** every SQL must begin "Whether..." and pose a yes/no question of law (not fact). It must identify the lower court's specific error + the statute/principle violated + the consequence.
- **Standard closing items** before PRAYER:
  - "The Appellants have not filed any other Appeal in the subject matter either before this Hon'ble Court or before Supreme Court of India."
  - "The Appellants craves leave to add or amend the Appeal if necessary. The Appellant craves leave to file more documents if necessary."
  - "The Appellants undertakes to file the translated copies of the vernacular documents as and when directed by this Hon'ble Court."
- **PRAYER pattern for SA** (more elaborate than Cri Appeal):
  a. Call records and proceedings
  b. Set aside impugned order(s) — citing annexure for each
  c. Remand OR alternatively decree (the "OR alternative" branch is critical)
  d. Grant stay
  e. Grant ex-parte ad-interim relief
  f. Other appropriate relief (catchall)
