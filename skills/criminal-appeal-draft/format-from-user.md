Structural reference compiled from the user-supplied master Criminal Appeal template (with Suspension + Condonation of Delay).

## ① MAIN APPEAL — exact format pattern

```
IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT BOMBAY BENCH AT NAGPUR.

CRIMINAL APPEAL NO._______/<YEAR>

APPELLANT:-
<Name> S/o / D/o <Father's Name>,
Aged about <age> years, Occ.: <occupation>, R/o. <address>
[(In Jail) — append where applicable]


...VERSUS…


RESPONDENT	:	State of Maharashtra,
		Through Police Station, <PS name>, Tahsil <T>, District. <D>.


CRIMINAL APPEAL UNDER SECTION 374(2) OF CODE OF CRIMINAL PROCEDURE
READ WITH SECTION 415 (2) OF THE BHARTIYA NAGARIK SURAKSHA SANHITA, 2023.


	Being aggrieved by the Judgment and order dated <date> passed by the learned <lower-court designation> in <case-no> thereby, sentencing and convicting the appellants by passing following Operative Order: -

[BULLETED OPERATIVE ORDER RECITAL — exact paragraphs from impugned order]
  •	Accused <Name> is convicted under Section 235(2) of the Code of Criminal Procedure for the offence punishable under Sections <list> of the IPC and section <list> of the <Special Act>. <Where applicable: section-42-POCSO note about higher-degree absorption.>

  •	That, the accused <Name> is sentenced to undergo rigorous imprisonment for <N> (<words>) years and to pay fine of Rs.<amount>/- ... for the offence punishable under Section <s> of <statute>. In default of payment of fine amount, he shall further undergo rigorous imprisonment for <N> (<words>) <month/year>.

  [Additional sentencing paragraphs as per impugned order]

  •	As the accused has been sentenced for the offence punishable under section <s> of <statute> and similar punishment is provided for the offence under section <s> of <other statute>, no separate sentence is imposed on the accused under section <s> of <other statute>. <Similarly clauses for absorption.>

  •	That, all the sentences imposed shall run concurrently.

  •	That, the accused <Name> is entitled for set off under Section 428 of the Cr.P.C. for the period undergone by him in jail.
  •	That the accused <Name> is acquitted under Section 235(1) of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 of the offence punishable under Section <s> of <statute>. (where partial acquittal)

  •	That, the seized muddemal property i.e. <items> being worthless be destroyed, after appeal period is over.

  •	That, the copy of the judgment be supplied to the convicted accused free of costs.

  •	In depositing fine amount by the accused, it be paid to victim girl (name and address as per charge-sheet) through her father towards compensation. The victim is also entitled for separate compensation under section 357-A of Cr.P.C. as per her entitlement.

  •	The D.L.S.A., <district> is recommended to pay compensation to the child within the ambit of Section 357 A(2) of the Code of Criminal Procedure and Section 9(2) of the POCSO Rules ... (POCSO-specific clauses where applicable)

  •	Copy of the judgment be sent to DLSA, <district>.

  •	The copy of Judgment and order dated <date> passed by the learned <lower-court> in <case-no> is annexed herewith and marked as ANNEXURE-A.

  •	The Appellant begs to file the present memo of appeal on the following facts and grounds.


F A C T S

	The prosecution case in nutshell is as under: -

  •	[Chronological narrative paragraph 1 — establishing the parties and date/place context.]

  •	[Continued narrative — alleged events, with dates. Each paragraph one event.]

  •	[Inline annexure markers for documentary evidence within narrative: "the gist of the Chargesheet in <case-no> is marked and annexed herewith as ANNEXURE-A" etc.]

  •	[Witness list paragraph — "The prosecution examined <N> witnesses in support of its case, including PW-1 (Victim), PW-2 (Father)..."]

  •	[Defense paragraph — "The Appellant's defence, raised under Section 313 Cr.P.C. and through cross-examination, was of total denial and false implication due to <reason>."]

  •	By the impugned Judgment and Order dated <date>, the learned Trial Court <action — e.g., "acquitted the Appellant of Section <s> IPC but convicted him for the attempt to commit aggravated penetrative sexual assault under Section <s> r/w Section 511 IPC and related offences, relying primarily on the oral testimony of the child witnesses despite the absence of medical corroboration."> [case-specific summary]


G R O U N D S

  •	The learned Special Court erred in law and facts of the case and failed to appreciate the evidence in proper perspective and wrongly convicted the appellant in absence of substantial evidence.

  •	[Substantive case-specific grounds — each as a numbered/bulleted paragraph of substantial prose. Each ground:
     - identifies the trial court's specific error
     - explains why it's an error (legal principle + how facts deviate)
     - cites the consequence
     - references specific PW numbers, paragraph numbers from impugned judgment, or annexures]

  •	[Standard ground variants seen in master:
     - "failed to appreciate the fatal admission regarding <issue>"
     - "ingredients of Section <s> were not made out"
     - "failed to appreciate the lack of Independent Witnesses"
     - "conduct of the Prosecution Witnesses is unnatural and unreliable"
     - "failed to consider the Defense of <X>"
     - "Identity of the Accused was not established in accordance with law"
     - "erred in relying on Child Witnesses without sufficient corroboration"
     - "Sentence imposed is harsh and disproportionate"
     - "failed to appreciate that the defence has been able to rebut statutory presumption"]

  •	That the judgment of conviction passed by the learned trial Court is illegal, erroneous and liable to be set aside.

  •	That the appellants reserve its rights to raise additional grounds challenging the order of conviction during the course of hearing.

  •	That, the appellants have filed instant criminal appeal within limitation and has not challenged the impugned judgment and order before the Hon'ble Supreme Court of India or this Hon'ble High Court at any time earlier.


PRAYER-

It is, therefore, prayed that this Hon'ble Court may kindly be pleased to:

a.
Quash and set aside the Judgment and Order of conviction dated <date> passed by the learned <lower-court> in <case-no> and further be pleased to ACQUIT the Appellant/Accused <Name> from the offences charged against him;

b.
Grant any other relief deems fit just and proper in the facts and circumstances of the case in favour of the appellant.


NAGPUR		                   (<COUNSEL NAME>, Adv.)
DATE:   .__.<YEAR>                      COUNSEL FOR APPELLANT


[OPTIONAL — where applicable:]
Note: The Appellant is in jail therefore his affidavit may kindly be dispensed with.


[OPTIONAL — Notes on Limitation block where appeal late:]
Notes on Limitation:
  •	Order of impugned order – <date>.
  •	Last day for filing of appeal – __.__.<YEAR>.
  •	Appeal filed on ________ (the application for condonation of delay is filed separately).
  •	Delay in filing appeal _______ days.
```

		Through Police Station, <PS>, Tahsil <T>, District. <D>.

AND 430 (1) OF THE BHARATIYA NAGARIK SURAKSHA SANHITA, 2023
FOR SUSPENSION OF SENTENCE AND FOR BAIL.

  •	That the appellant has filed present appeal challenging the judgment and order dated <date> passed by the learned <lower-court> in <case-no> thereby, sentencing and convicting the appellant by passing following Operative Order: -

[REPEAT BULLETED OPERATIVE ORDER PARAGRAPHS — same as in main appeal]

  •	That, on perusal of the accompanying appeal memo this Hon'ble Court will be pleased to find that the appellant has demonstrated substantial ground on both fact and law for kind consideration of this Hon'ble Court. The appellant craves leave of this Hon'ble Court to take into consideration the ground raised in appeal memo while deciding the present application.

  •	It is submitted that, the Appellant has made out good prima case and there is every possibility that he will succeed before this Hon'ble Court finally.

  •	It is submitted that the present Appellant has been convicted for <sentence summary>. It is most humbly and respectfully submitted that, looking to the pendency of the matter before this Hon'ble Court there is no possibility of appeal of the Appellant being taken for final hearing in future.

  •	That the interest of justice, demands that during the pendency of present appeal, it is necessary to suspend the substantial jail sentence of the appellant as imposed by the learned <lower-court> on such terms and conditions as this Hon'ble Court deems fit.

  •	It is submitted that the present Appellant is <age description> and is <employment / family status>. <Family circumstances paragraph — case-specific.>

  •	The Appellant is having deep root in the society and as such, there is no possibility of Appellant to run away from the course of justice.   •	The Appellant has not filed any other application for grant of bail in the present matter either before this Court or Hon'ble Supreme Court.

 	Hence, this application.

a.
Suspend the substantive jail sentence as imposed by the learned <lower-court> vide judgment dated <date> in <case-no> and release the Appellant on bail forthwith on such terms and conditions as this Hon'ble Court may deemed fit during the pendency of the appeal, in the interest of justice;

## SECTION ORDER IN FINAL .docx

1. Index
2. Synopsis
3. Main Appeal (Cause Title → Parties → Statutory Opening → "Being aggrieved" opener → Operative Order Recital → ANNEXURE-A intro → "Appellant begs to file" → FACTS → GROUNDS → PRAYER → Counsel block → Notes/Limitation)
4. List of Annexures
5. Suspension of Sentence Application (always)
6. Condonation of Delay Application (only if filed late)

Each section starts with cause title repeated. Page-break between sections.

## NOTES FOR DRAFTER

- **Parties separator:** `...VERSUS…` (three dots, all caps "VERSUS", em-dash style). Master uses this style throughout.
- **Court header:** "IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT BOMBAY BENCH AT NAGPUR." (period at end).
- **Counsel block:** counsel name in parens BEFORE "COUNSEL FOR APPELLANT" line.
- **Operative Order Recital is repeated** in main appeal + Suspension app + Condonation app — copy verbatim across all three.
- **Inline annexure markers:** ALWAYS bold-able and uppercase: ANNEXURE-A, ANNEXURE-B (Colly), etc.
- **The "(Colly)" suffix** is for collective annexures (multiple documents like depositions of N witnesses grouped as one).
- **Standard closing grounds (3 paragraphs)** appear in this order:
  1. "judgment of conviction is illegal, erroneous and liable to be set aside"
  2. "reserve rights to raise additional grounds during course of hearing"
  3. "filed within limitation, not approached SC or this HC earlier"
- **Standard prayer always includes:** (a) primary relief specific to appeal + (b) catchall "Grant any other relief deems fit just and proper..."
- **POCSO-specific clauses** appear in operative order recital where the impugned conviction is under POCSO Act — preserve verbatim from impugned order.
- **Notes on Limitation block** appears only when appeal is filed beyond 60-day limitation. Format above.
