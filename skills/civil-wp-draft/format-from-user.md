## ① MAIN PETITION — exact format pattern

```
IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT BOMBAY BENCH AT NAGPUR

WRIT PETITION No.                 /<YEAR>
ACT CODE -

(In the matter of challenge to <action being challenged — e.g.,
"the judgments and decree dated <date> passed by learned <appellate court>
in dismissing the <appeal-no> and confirming judgment and decree passed by
learned <trial court> dated <date> in <suit-no>, filed by the Respondent
for <relief> under section <s> of the <statute>.">)

PETITIONER:-
<Name> S/o / D/o <Father's Name>
Aged <age> years, Occupation – <occupation>,
Address – <full address>

///VERSUS///

RESPONDENT:-

<Name>,
Aged about <age> years, Occ. <occupation>, R/o <address>


WRIT PETITION UNDER ARTICLE 226 AND 227 OF THE CONSTITUTION OF INDIA


 		The petitioner most humbly begs to submits as under-
 	That being aggrieved by <impugned action — full descriptor>. The copy of <impugned order> dated <date> passed by the learned <court> in <case-no> is marked and annexed herewith as ANNEXURE-A. The Petitioner is filing this instant writ petition on the following facts and grounds.


F A C T S

  •	[Opening fact paragraph identifying parties' positions in lower court — e.g., "The Respondent was the original Plaintiff and the Petitioner was the original defendant in <case-no> before the learned <trial court> (Parties are hereinafter referred to as 'Plaintiff' and 'Defendant')."]

  •	[Plaintiff's case in trial court — paragraph summarising plaintiff's pleadings and reliefs sought.]

  •	[Plaintiff's pleadings in detail — multiple paragraphs as needed. Each substantive document referenced inline:
      "The copy of plaint dated <date> in <case-no> filed by the plaintiff is marked and annexed herewith as ANNEXURE-B."]

  •	[Defendant's written statement summary — paragraph(s).]

  •	[Defendant's specific defenses with documentary references:
      "The copy of written statement filed by the defendant in <case-no> dated <date> is marked and annexed herewith as ANNEXURE-C."]

  •	[Issues framed by trial court — single paragraph + ANNEXURE-D]

  •	[Plaintiff's evidence — deposition + documents:
      "Plaintiff entered into witness box and examined himself as PW-1 (Exh.<N>). The copy of deposition of plaintiff (PW-1) dated <date> is marked and annexed herewith as ANNEXURE-E."
      "Plaintiff has placed on record documents vide list of documents dated <date>. The copies of list of documents dated <date> along with documents are marked and annexed herewith as ANNEXURE-F (Colly.)"]

  •	[Defendant's evidence — same pattern. ANNEXURE-G + H (Colly).]

  •	[Trial court judgment + ANNEXURE-I]

  •	[First appeal narrative — defendant filed Regular Civil Appeal, applications during appeal, etc.]

  •	[Multiple inline annexures for orders rejecting interim applications during appeal — ANNEXURE-J, K, L, M, N as needed.]

  •	[First appellate court judgment + reference back to ANNEXURE-A for the impugned final order.]

 	Being aggrieved by <combined impugned orders with annexure refs>.


G R O U N D S

  •	That the learned trial Court as well as learned First Appellate Court erred in law and also in fact, in passing the decree in favour of plaintiff.

  •	[Substantive case-specific grounds. Each begins with: "That the learned <court> erred in <action>" or "That the learned <court> failed to appreciate <legal principle>" or "That the learned <court> should have <correct action>".]

  •	[Where citations are invoked: "in view of judgment of Hon'ble Apex Court in case of <Name v. Name> (<citation>)" — but only if citation has been verified, never fabricate.]

  •	Otherwise also judgment passed by the learned First Appellate Court and learned trial Court is illegal, erroneous and liable to be set aside.

  •	[Stay-relevant ground where stay sought: "That the suit premises is the only place where defendant is doing his business... if executed, the defendant and his family will be on street. Therefore it is necessary in the interest of justice to grant stay..."]

  •	The learned counsel undertakes to file English translation of the Marathi documents if any or as and when directed by this Hon'ble Court.

  •	The petitioner is not having any other alternate as much as equally effective remedy than to approach this Hon'ble court for the redressal of their grievances.

  •	[Caveat handling where applicable: "Petitioner has received notice of caveat from Advocate of respondent, petitioner undertakes to serve notice of instant petition on the learned Advocate for the respondent in caveat."]

  •	The petitioner crave the leave of this Hon'ble Court to alter/amend the above grounds as and when required.


PRAYER-

In view of the facts and circumstances mentioned above and the grounds raised herein, the petitioner most humbly prays that this Hon'ble Court may graciously be pleased to:


a.
Call for the records and proceedings of <suit-no> from the file of the learned <trial court> and record of <appeal-no> from the learned <appellate court>;

b.
Quash and set aside the impugned judgment and decree dated <date> passed by the learned <trial court> passed in <suit-no> and judgment and decree passed by the learned <appellate court> in <appeal-no> dated <date>;

c.
[Primary substantive relief — e.g., "To dismiss <suit-no> with costs throughout;"]
ALTERNATIVELY
[Alternative relief — e.g., "To allow applications Exhs.<N> and <N> filed by the appellant in <appeal-no> and to remand <suit-no> for decision as per provisions of law;"]

d.
To grant stay to the effect and operation of judgment and decree passed by the learned <courts>, till the decision of the instant petition;

[Sub-clause:]
To grant ad-interim relief in terms of prayer clause-d;

e.
Pass any such other and further orders as this Hon'ble Court may deem fit and proper in the facts and circumstances of the present case, to meet the ends of justice.


NAGPUR
Dated-__/__/<YEAR>
COUNSEL FOR PETITIONER
<COUNSEL NAME>
```

## NOTES FOR DRAFTER

- **Parties terminology:** PETITIONER / RESPONDENT (not Appellant/Applicant).
- **Parties separator:** `///VERSUS///` (uppercase) in main petition; `///Versus///` (mixed case) in Index/Synopsis/List.
- **Court header:** "IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT BOMBAY BENCH AT NAGPUR" (NO period at end — distinct from Cri Appeal).
- **Statutory opening:** "CONSTITUTION" — note the correct spelling (a common typo is "CONSTITUION" without the second "T"; the Registry rejects pleadings with this misspelling).
- **Salutation:** "The petitioner most humbly begs to submits as under-" (single line, hyphen ending).
- **Filing intent line** (after ANNEXURE-A intro): "The Petitioner is filing this instant writ petition on the following facts and grounds."
- **Grounds open broadly:** "That the learned trial Court as well as learned First Appellate Court erred in law and also in fact..."
- **Prayer pattern is more elaborate than appeals:** includes primary relief + ALTERNATIVELY clause + stay + ad-interim sub-clause + catchall.
- **The "ALTERNATIVELY" branch** is critical for civil WP — captures the alternative relief if main is not granted.
- **"Caveat undertaking" clause** appears where opposing side has filed caveat — required for WP under HC rules.
- **"Equally effective remedy"** clause is mandatory — Article 226 maintainability requires no alternative remedy.
- **Counsel block** (Sunil format): "COUNSEL FOR PETITIONER" line BEFORE counsel name (different from Cri Appeal where counsel name comes first).
- **Inline annexure markers** can run very high — Sunil master has A through N (14 annexures). Drafter must keep track precisely.
- **Each documentary reference** uses pattern: "The copy of <doc> dated <date> is marked and annexed herewith as ANNEXURE-<X>" — exact phrasing.
