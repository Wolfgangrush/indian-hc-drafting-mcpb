```
IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT BOMBAY NAGPUR BENCH, NAGPUR

CRIMINAL APPLICATION (APL) NO. ___/ <YEAR>

ACT CODE-

In the matter of Section 528 of the Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita, 2023 r/w Section 482 of the Code of Criminal Procedure seeking quashing of the proceedings in Crime No. <N>, registered with Police Station <PS>, Dist. <D>, for the Offences punishable under Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023)

APPLICANTS
1.
<Name 1>, Aged about: <age> years, Occ: <occupation>,
R/o: <address>


2.
<Name 2>, Age: <age> years, Occ: <occupation>,
R/o: <address>

[... additional applicants as numbered entries — APL applications often have multiple applicants
   (family groups, society members, co-accused). Each numbered separately.]


///VERSUS///
NON- APPLICANTS
1.
State of Maharashtra Through Police Station Officer, Police Station <PS>, District <D>.


2.
<Complainant Name>, Age: <age> years, Occ: <occupation>, R/o: <address>.


APPLICATION UNDER SECTION 528 OF THE BHARATIYA NAGARIK SURAKSHA SANHITA, 2023 R/W SECTION 482 FOR QUASHING OF FIR

 	The Applicants most humbly and respectfully submit as under-

  •	The Applicants are law-abiding citizens with no criminal antecedents and are permanent residents of the addresses mentioned above.

  •	[Identity / role context paragraph — establishes legitimate position of applicants:
      e.g., "They are the lawful purchasers and family members of the bona fide plot owners ...
      having acquired their plots through legally executed and registered sale deeds."]

  •	The Applicants are being falsely implicated in Crime No. <N>, filed on <date> at <time>, registered with Non-Applicant No.<N> (Police Station <PS>) for the offences punishable under Sections <list> of the BNS, 2023. A copy of the FIR No. <N> dated <date> is marked and annexed as ANNEXURE-A.

  •	The story of the prosecution as reflected from the FIR is as under:
  •	[Prosecution narrative paragraphs — chronological summary from FIR. Use "It is alleged" framing.]


  •	It is submitted that, the allegations in the FIR even if taken on the face value are entirely baseless and are afterthought in nature and the dispute is of strictly civil in nature regarding <description of underlying civil dispute>.

  •	[Detailed factual rebuttal — establishing Applicants' lawful position, with documentary support:
      "It is pertinent to note that on <date>, the Applicants were lawfully present on their own property...
      The copy of the sale-deed, the stamp duty, the 7/12 extract are hereby marked and annexed as ANNEXURE-B(Colly)."]

  •	[Counter-narrative — what actually happened from Applicants' view:
      "It was actually the Complainant ... and his faction ... who formed an unlawful assembly and viciously attacked the Applicants ..."
      Where there's a counter-FIR by Applicants:
      "Applicant No. <N> (<Name>) had already registered an FIR against the Complainant's faction on the exact same day. FIR No. <N>/<YEAR> was registered at <time>, whereas the present impugned FIR No. <N> was registered hours later at <time> as a malicious, retaliatory counter-blast. A copy of FIR No. <N>/<YEAR> on the same date is annexed hereto as ANNEXURE-C."]

  •	[Detailed sequence-of-events paragraphs — what really occurred, person by person, hour by hour. Each substantive paragraph as a separate bullet.]

  •	[Where relevant — supplementary documents establishing background:
      "Long before this physical altercation, ... had already submitted a detailed written clarification on <date> to the Sub-Registrar and Police, exposing that <facts>. The copy of written clarification dated <date> is hereby marked and annexed as ANNEXURE-D."]

  •	That by perusal of the FIR it can be ascertained that the allegations in the FIR even if taken on the face value are entirely baseless, afterthought and the dispute is strictly civil in nature regarding <description>.

  •	The Applicants are falsely implicated and pray for the quashing of the FIR relying on the following grounds without causing prejudice to each other:


G R O U N D S

  •	That, the FIR No. <N>/<YEAR> is attended with gross mala fides and has been maliciously instituted with an ulterior motive for wreaking vengeance on the Applicants. <Substantive ground prose — e.g., counter-blast theory, timeline contradiction, mens rea analysis>.

  •	That, the genesis of the alleged physical altercation is <a property dispute / civil-nature dispute>. Under Section 9 of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, the Civil Courts have the exclusive jurisdiction to try all suits of a civil nature, including suits where the right to property is contested. <Why criminal forum is wrong / civil remedy was available>. The Non-Applicant ... has maliciously bypassed the Civil Courts, taking the law into their own hands ... and is now abusing the criminal machinery to settle a civil property dispute.

  •	It is submitted that, the allegations made in the FIR fail to qualify the tests laid down by the Hon'ble Apex Court for quashing criminal proceedings, particularly as established in the landmark case of State of Haryana v. Bhajan Lal (1992 Supp (1) SCC 335).

  •	That, the continuation of the proceedings against the Applicant would be a gross abuse of the process of the Court. The Applicants are respectable citizens and members of society. To compel them to face the trial based on flimsy, omnibus, and vindictive allegations, would result in a manifest miscarriage of justice.

  •	That, by perusing the entire FIR, it is to be noted that no offence is made out against the Applicants.

  •	The Applicants crave leave to add, alter, or amend the grounds as and when required during the course of the hearing.

  •	The Applicants confirm that they have not approached any other Court, including the Hon'ble Supreme Court of India, for the reliefs prayed for in this Application.


PRAYER-

It is therefore most respectfully prayed that this Hon'ble Court may be pleased to:


a.
Quash and set aside the impugned F.I.R. No. <N> dated <date> registered at Police Station <PS>, District <D>, against the present Applicants for the offences punishable under Sections <list> of the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023;

b.
Pending the hearing and final disposal of this Criminal Application, stay the investigation and all further proceedings arising out of Crime No. <N>/<YEAR> dated <date> registered at Police Station <PS>;

c.
Direct the Non-Applicant No. <N> (State) not to take any coercive action, including arrest, against the Applicants in connection with F.I.R. No. <N> dated <date>;

d.
Grant ad-interim relief in terms of prayer clauses (b) and (c) above;

e.
Grant any other and further relief as this Hon'ble Court may deem fit and proper in the facts and circumstances of the case, in the interest of justice.


NAGPUR DATED-__.__.<YEAR>
COUNSEL FOR APPLICANTS
```

## NOTES FOR DRAFTER

- **Parties terminology:** APPLICANTS / NON-APPLICANTS (often plural — APL apps frequently have multiple applicants and multiple non-applicants).
- **Parties separator:** `///VERSUS///` (uppercase, triple-slash).
- **Court header:** "IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT BOMBAY NAGPUR BENCH, NAGPUR" — note "NAGPUR BENCH, NAGPUR" sequence (distinct from ABA's "NAGPUR BENCH NAGPUR" without comma, distinct from appeals' "BENCH AT NAGPUR").
- **Cause title parenthetical:** "In the matter of Section 528 of the Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita, 2023 r/w Section 482 of the Code of Criminal Procedure seeking quashing of <action>" — descriptive recital appears BEFORE party block (different from appeals where it follows case-no line).
- **Statutory opening:** "APPLICATION UNDER SECTION 528 OF THE BHARATIYA NAGARIK SURAKSHA SANHITA, 2023 R/W SECTION 482 FOR QUASHING OF FIR" — S.528 BNSS = inherent powers (= S.482 CrPC).
- **Salutation:** "The Applicants most humbly and respectfully submit as under-"
- **HAS GROUNDS section** (unlike ABA which has none). Distinct heading "G R O U N D S" with spaced letters.
- **Standard quashing grounds:**
  1. Mala fides / counter-blast theory
  2. Civil dispute disguised as criminal (S.9 CPC + S.34 SRA references)
  3. Bhajan Lal test invocation: "fail to qualify the tests laid down by the Hon'ble Apex Court for quashing criminal proceedings, particularly as established in the landmark case of State of Haryana v. Bhajan Lal (1992 Supp (1) SCC 335)" — this citation is verified, can be cited.
  4. Abuse of process
  5. No offence made out
  6. Reservation to add/amend grounds
  7. First approach to court / no parallel proceedings
- **Prayer clauses** (typical 5):
  - (a) Quash FIR
  - (b) Stay investigation/proceedings during pendency
  - (c) Direction against coercive action / arrest
  - (d) Ad-interim relief
  - (e) Catchall
- **Counsel block** (the 482 APL master format — different from ABA): just "COUNSEL FOR APPLICANTS" line below NAGPUR DATED-__.__.<YEAR> — counsel name MISSING in master file. **Drafter MUST add counsel name in same line or below as per the user's convention.** Other formats (the ABA master format, the Civil WP master format) have counsel name explicitly. This master may be incomplete — flag to the user for verification.
- **Inline annexure markers** common in APL: A=impugned FIR, B (Colly)=lawful-position documents (sale deeds, registers, etc.), C=counter-FIR (where applicable), D=prior representations/notices, E onward=additional supporting documents.
- **Bhajan Lal citation IS approved for use** (verified case law — 1992 Supp (1) SCC 335). Drafter may cite this without the user supplying PDF.
- **No "in jail" note** — APL applicants typically not in custody.
- **No Notes on Limitation** — APL is not time-limited (subject to delay arguments by State).
