## ① MAIN APPLICATION — exact format pattern

```
IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT BOMBAY BENCH AT NAGPUR

Criminal Revision Application No.                 /<YEAR>
ACT CODE -

(In the matter of challenge to the order dated <date> passed by learned <Sessions Judge / lower court>, <D> in <Sessions Trial No. / case-no> passed below Exh.<N>, whereby the learned <court> <action — e.g., "allowed the application filed by the prosecution for recall of PW<N>, namely <Name> at belated stage and for filling up lacuna">)


APPLICANT

<Name> S/o / D/o <Father's Name>
Aged <age> years, Occupation – <occupation> [(in Jail) where applicable],
Address – <full address>


///Versus///


NON-APPLICANT

The State of Maharashtra, through Police Station Officer, Police Station <PS>, Tahsil <T> Dist. <D>


CRIMINAL REVISION APPLICATION UNDER SECTION 442 OF BHARATIYA NAGARIK SURAKSHA SANHITA, 2023

The Applicant most humbly begs to submits as under-
 	That being aggrieved by the order dated <date> passed by learned <Sessions Judge>, <D> in <case-no> passed below Exh.<N>, whereby the learned <court> <action>, the Applicant is filing this instant Criminal Revision Application on the following facts and grounds.


F A C T S

  •	That the Applicant is the accused no. <N> in <Sessions Trial No.>, pending before the learned <Principal Sessions and District Judge>, <D> for the offence punishable under sections <list> of <statute>, r/w sections <list> of <other statute>.

  •	That the case of prosecution in short is that, on <date>, [chronological narrative of alleged incident — single dense paragraph]. The gist of the Chargesheet in <case-no> is marked and annexed herewith as ANNEXURE-A.

  •	[Procedural history paragraphs — each major step in trial as a bullet, with annexure where documents are referenced:
      "That during the course of trial, the learned <court> framed charges, Applicant and other accused pleaded not guilty. In support of case, the prosecution examined <person> as PW-<N>. PW-<N>, has [supported/not supported] the prosecution. The prosecution conducted cross examination of PW-<N>. The copy of deposition of PW-<N> is annexed herewith as ANNEXURE-B."]

  •	[Inline annexure markers continue: ANNEXURE-C (related application), ANNEXURE-D (lower court order), ANNEXURE-E (related affidavit), ANNEXURE-F (recall application), ANNEXURE-G (impugned order), ANNEXURE-H (further depositions), ANNEXURE-I, etc.]

  •	[Closing fact paragraph reasserting impugned order:]
 	That being aggrieved by the order dated <date> passed by learned <court> in <case-no> passed below Exh.<N>, whereby the learned <court> <action>, the Applicant is filing this instant Criminal Revision Application on the following grounds without causing prejudice to each other.


G R O U N D S

  •	[Substantive case-specific grounds. Each ground:
     - identifies the lower court's specific error
     - explains the legal principle violated
     - cites the consequence
     - is structured as a complete prose paragraph (not bullet sub-points)]

  •	[Standard ground patterns observed in master:
     - "the learned <court> failed to appreciate that <factual point>"
     - "the learned <court> erred in exercising its jurisdiction under Section <s> of the <statute>. While Section <s> empowers the Court to <action>, this power is strictly conditioned upon <conditions>."
     - "the learned <court> failed to consider a glaring evidentiary gap. <factual gap>"
     - "the impugned order violates the fundamental right of the Applicant to a fair trial. <how>"]

  •	That, except the present Application the Applicant has not filed any other application in the subject matter of review either before this Hon'ble Court or the Hon'ble Apex Court and no any application/appeal is pending before any Court of law.

  •	The Applicant craves leave of this Hon'ble Court to add, amend, or alter any of the grounds raised hereinabove at the time of the hearing


PRAYER-

In view of the facts and circumstances mentioned above and the grounds raised herein, the Applicant most humbly prays that this Hon'ble Court may graciously be pleased to:


a.
Call for the records and proceedings of <case-no> from the file of the learned <court>, and after examining the legality, propriety, and correctness of the impugned order, be pleased to allow the present Criminal Revision Application;

b.
Quash and set aside the impugned order dated <date> passed by the learned <court>, below Exh.<N> in <case-no>, and consequently, <consequential relief — e.g., "reject the application filed by the prosecution under Section <s> of <statute> for the recall of PW<N> (<Name>)">;

c.
By way of Interim Relief, stay the effect, operation, to the proceedings of <case-no>, pending the hearing and final disposal of the present Criminal Revision Application;

d.
Pass any such other and further orders as this Hon'ble Court may deem fit and proper in the facts and circumstances of the present case, to meet the ends of justice.


NAGPUR
Dated-__/__/<YEAR>
COUNSEL FOR APPLICANT
<COUNSEL NAME>
```

## NOTES FOR DRAFTER

- **Parties terminology:** APPLICANT / NON-APPLICANT (matches civil application style).
- **Parties separator:** `///Versus///` (mixed case, triple-slash).
- **Court header:** "IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT BOMBAY BENCH AT NAGPUR" (NO period — same as Civil WP, distinct from Cri Appeal which has period).
- **Case type line:** "Criminal Revision Application No." — note Title Case (mixed), not all-caps. Distinct from "CRIMINAL APPEAL" all-caps style.
- **Cause title parenthetical:** "(In the matter of challenge to the order dated <date> ...)" — appears BEFORE party block. Long descriptive recital.
- **⚠️ STATUTORY OPENING TYPO ALERT:** master uses "BHARTIYA NYAYA SANHITA" but S.442 may belong to BNSS (Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita = procedural code) not BNS (Nyaya Sanhita = substantive). Verify with the user on first use. Possible alternatives:
  - "CRIMINAL REVISION APPLICATION UNDER SECTION 442 OF BHARATIYA NAGARIK SURAKSHA SANHITA, 2023" (likely correct)
  - "CRIMINAL REVISION APPLICATION UNDER SECTIONS 397 AND 401 OF THE CODE OF CRIMINAL PROCEDURE, 1973" (well-established CrPC equivalent)
- **Salutation:** "The Applicant most humbly begs to submits as under-"
- **"Being aggrieved" appears twice** — once at salutation (intro to filing) and again at end of FACTS as closing/transition before GROUNDS. Both required per master.
- **GROUNDS structure for revision** is distinctive — focuses on supervisory jurisdiction errors:
  - Belated procedural moves by lower court / prosecution
  - Jurisdictional limits of statutory provisions invoked
  - Evidentiary gaps the lower court ignored
  - Fair trial / fundamental rights violations
  - NOT factual reappreciation (revision is not appeal)
- **Prayer clauses** (4 standard):
  - (a) Call records and proceedings + examine legality
  - (b) Quash impugned order + consequential relief
  - (c) Stay interim relief
  - (d) Catchall
- **Counsel block** (the Criminal Revision master format): "COUNSEL FOR APPLICANT" line BEFORE counsel name (which appears as bare initials, e.g., "[counsel initials]" without "Adv." suffix in this template). Other formats use "(Name, Adv.)". the user may want to standardize — preserve master's variation but flag.
- **No "in jail" note typically** for revision applicants (often on bail or pending trial).
- **No Notes on Limitation block.** Revision has its own limitation under S.397/442 (typically 90 days) but is rarely formally noted in the document.
- **Revision is SUPERVISORY** — Drafter must NOT frame grounds as factual reappreciation. Focus on jurisdictional excess, illegality, irregularity, or fundamental injustice.
