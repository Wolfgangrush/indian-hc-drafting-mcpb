## ① MAIN APPLICATION — exact format pattern

```
IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT BOMBAY, NAGPUR BENCH NAGPUR

CRIMINAL APPLICATION (ABA) No.             /<YEAR>

APPLICANT:	       <Name> S/o / D/o <Father's Name>
	        Aged about <age> year, occupation
 	        <occupation>, R/o <village>, Tah-<T>
                                         District-<D>

..VERSUS..

NON-APPLICANT:	 State of Maharashtra,
Through Police Station Officer, Police Station, <PS>, District <D>.


APPLICATION UNDER SECTION 482 OF BHARATIYA NAGARIK SURAKSHA SANHITA, FOR GRANT OF ANTICIPATORY BAIL

	The applicant above named most humbly and respectfully beg to submits as under:-

That the Applicant is permanent resident of given address in the cause title. Applicant is peace loving and law abiding citizen and having deep roots in the society.

 That, Crime is registered against the present applicant at Police Station <PS> District <D>, for the offences punishable under section <list> of <Indian Penal Code / Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita> Vide FIR No <N>/<YEAR>. The copy of FIR vide Crime No.<N>/<YEAR> dated <date> is marked and annexed herewith as ANNEXURE-A.

 That in brief case of the prosecution as revealed from the FIR is as under:

  [Prosecution narrative paragraph(s) — chronological summary of allegations from FIR. Use "alleged" / "It is alleged" framing throughout. Single dense paragraph per allegation cluster.]


It is submitted that, no incident has occurred as alleged in the FIR. It is further submitted that, even if entire contents in FIR are accepted as it is, it will not make out any case against the applicant particularly for the offence under section <s> of <statute> as <reason — e.g., "there is unexplained and inordinate delay in filing FIR.">

 [Defense narrative paragraphs — explanation of why allegations are false / counter-version of events. Each as a paragraph. Common patterns:
   - workplace dispute / professional rivalry
   - prior litigation / counter-FIR by applicant
   - civil dispute disguised as criminal
   - personal grudge / political motive
   - delay in FIR registration vs alleged incident
   - absence of medical evidence
   - improbability of alleged events]

 [Where there's an earlier related anticipatory bail / regular bail order — referenced inline:
  "That the Applicant was released on anticipatory bail in connection with FIR vide bearing No. <N>/<YEAR> registered with Police Station, <PS> for the offence punishable under section <s> ..., by the learned. Dist. & Sessions Judge, <district> in <case-no> dated <date>. The copy of Anticipatory Bail order ... is marked and annexed herewith as Annexure-B."]

 [Where applicant first approached lower court and was rejected — referenced:
  "That after registration of FIR Dated <date> ... the applicant apprehending his arrest has filed anticipatory Bail application before the Hon'ble District and sessions Judge <D>. The copy of the bail application bearing bail application No.<N>/<YEAR> filed by the applicant <date> is marked and annexed herewith as Annexure-C."
  + Respondent's reply as Annexure-D
  + Lower court rejection order as Annexure-E]


 Applicant is peace loving citizen and all the movable as well as immovable property of the applicant is within the jurisdiction of this Hon'ble Court and therefore there is no possibility of absconding and is ready to cooperate with the investigation. It is submitted that, applicant is no way concerned with the alleged offence mentioned in the FIR. It is further submitted that, even if entire contents in FIR are accepted as it is, it will not make out any case against the applicant.


That apprehending the arrest by the hands of the police with respect to Crime No.<N>/<YEAR> registered with Police Station, <PS> dated <date> the applicant is filling the instant Anticipatory Bail application.


However, looking to the circumstances, applicant is having well founded apprehension of arrest at the hands of non-applicant. Applicant is having his roots in the society. There is no chance of absconding of applicant.


In these circumstances, custodial interrogation of the applicant is not warranted. Applicant is duty bound to comply with the condition as may be imposed by this Hon'ble Court. Similarly, applicant is also ready to cooperate in the investigation and ready to appear as and when called by this Hon'ble Court.


 That, the ingredients of the provisions of <statute> are not made out and the present applicant is not responsible for alleged crime. Nothing is required to be recovered for the investigation of alleged crime. That the material investigation with respect to the present applicant is already completed.


In the aforesaid circumstances the false implication of the applicant cannot be ruled out and therefore it is prayed that the applicant may kindly be directed to be released on Anticipatory Bail in the event of his arrest.


 This is the first anticipatory bail application moved by the applicant and applicant has not approached any other Court for grant of anticipatory bail in this crime.

There is least possibility of fleeing away from the course of justice as applicant is having his respective families and business to maintain and look after.


		Hence, this application.


P R A Y E R:	It is, therefore, most humbly and respectfully prayed to this Hon'ble Court that this Hon'ble Court may be pleased to:-


[a)]
In the event of arrest of the applicant in respect of Crime No.<N>/<YEAR> dated <date> registered with Police Station, <PS>, Dist. <D>, for the offences punishable under <sections list of statute>, the applicant may kindly be released on anticipatory bail;

b)	ad-interim anticipatory bail in terms of prayer clause (a) may kindly be granted in favour of applicant, during the pendency of this application;

c)	grant any other relief which this Hon'ble Court deems fit just and proper, in the facts and circumstances of case.


NAGPUR			             COUNSEL FOR APPLICANT
DATE:__/__/<YEAR>	                   (<COUNSEL NAME>, Adv.)
```

## NOTES FOR DRAFTER

- **Parties terminology:** APPLICANT / NON-APPLICANT (not Petitioner/Respondent or Appellant).
- **Parties separator:** `..VERSUS..` (in main app) and `..Versus..` (in Index/Synopsis/List). Two dots both sides, lowercase variant for sub-pages.
- **Court header:** "IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT BOMBAY, NAGPUR BENCH NAGPUR" — note the comma + "NAGPUR BENCH NAGPUR" sequence (different from "BENCH AT NAGPUR" in appeals/WP).
- **Statutory opening:** "APPLICATION UNDER SECTION 482 OF BHARATIYA NAGARIK SURAKSHA SANHITA, FOR GRANT OF ANTICIPATORY BAIL" (S.482 BNSS = anticipatory bail, equivalent to S.438 CrPC). Some practitioners cite both; preserve master's choice.
- **Salutation:** "The applicant above named most humbly and respectfully beg to submits as under:-"
- **NO separate FACTS / GROUNDS sections.** ABA runs as a continuous narrative without these headings — fact paragraphs and legal arguments interleaved. Distinct from main appeals/petitions.
- **Standard ABA paragraph anchors** (usually appear in this rough order):
  1. Identity + character of applicant
  2. Crime registered (with FIR ANNEXURE-A intro)
  3. Prosecution case narrative (from FIR)
  4. Defense narrative (counter-version, denial, motive for false implication)
  5. References to prior bail orders (where any), counter-FIRs (where any) — each with annexure
  6. "Property within jurisdiction / no absconding"
  7. "Apprehending arrest"
  8. "Custodial interrogation not warranted"
  9. "Ingredients not made out"
  10. "First ABA / not approached other court"
  11. "Least possibility of fleeing"
  12. "Hence, this application."
- **Prayer clauses** (3 standard):
  - (a) Release on anticipatory bail
  - (b) Ad-interim anticipatory bail during pendency
  - (c) Catchall
- **Counsel block** (Anil format): "COUNSEL FOR APPLICANT" line BEFORE counsel name in parens.
- **PRAYER spacing:** master uses `P R A Y E R:` (with spaces) inline with content, not as a standalone heading. Preserve.
- **No Notes on Limitation block** — ABA isn't time-limited.
- **No "in jail" affidavit dispensation note** typically — ABA applicant is by definition NOT yet arrested.
- **Inline annexures** can include: FIR (A), prior bail orders (B), prior application (C), respondent's reply (D), impugned rejection order (E). More if multiple FIRs / orders involved.
